NSF The National Science Foundation and a private donor have awarded to the College funds totaling over $625,000 in support of these SPARC33 Scholarships. The SPARC3 STEM initiative was developed in 2009 by Gaston College to promote persistence and graduation rates of students taking STEM courses. It is widely acknowledged that the future economic prosperity of the U.S. is closely related to the success of students in the STEM fields.

Gaston College will award SPARC3 Scholarships of up to $5,600 per year per eligible student based on need as determined by your FASFA. Besides the financial support, there are many advantages to being a SPARC3 scholar. SPARC3 scholars will participate in activities such as unversity tours, museum trips and other STEM specific activies. They will also be encouraged to engage in undergraduate research experinces. SPARC3 scholars have personal academic mentors, and have early registration dates allowing them first choice of classes. Additionally, they are a cohort, and as such they take classes together, which encourages teamwork and group study. When preparing for transfer, SPARC3 scholars receive personal transfer advice to help them to select the university that best fits their needs and academic ability.

Eligibility

Applicants for the scholarship must meet the following eligibility requirements:

  • Must be at least 18 years of age
  • Must possess a high school diploma or equivalent
  • U.S. citizenship, nationality or lawful U.S. permanent residence
  • Full-time enrollment in the Associate of Science program at Gaston College
  • Demonstrated finacial need based upon federal guidelines
  • Overall unweighted GPA of 2.75 or higher
  • Submission of a letter of intent to complete the Associate of Science degree at Gaston College
  • Brief interview with SPARC3 program staff
